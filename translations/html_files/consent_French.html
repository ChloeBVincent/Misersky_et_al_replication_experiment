Why this study?
This study will give us an idea of how Dutch people estimate the proportion of men and women within different groups.

What will we ask of you during the study?
This study consists of two parts. In the first part, you will see various group names on the screen and will be asked to indicate how many men and how many women you think there actually are within that group in the Netherlands. For the second part, we will ask you a few questions about yourself, such as your age and gender identity.

How do we handle your data?
Processing your data helps us to analyse patterns in estimates. Your data will only be processed for scientific purposes and will under no circumstances be used for commercial purposes . All data is stored directly on a secure server at the University of Groningen and is processed completely anonymously. Research data that is published or made public will under no circumstances contain personal information and cannot be linked to you. Please be aware that your anonymous responses may be used for future research.

Do I have to take part in this study?
Participation in this study is voluntary. If you decide not to take part, you do not need to explain why, and this will not have any negative consequences for you. Your details cannot be deleted after participation, as all data is anonymous.

What else do you need to know?
Please do let us know if you have any questions or comments about this study. You can ask your questions now, during, or after the study has finished. You can do this by sending an email to the researcher responsible for this study: alexander . martin [at] rug . nl.

Do you have any questions or concerns about your rights as a participant or about the conduct of the research team? Please contact the Research Ethics Review Committee (CETO) of the Faculties of Arts, Philosophy, and Science and Engineering at the University of Groningen: ceto [at] rug . nl.
